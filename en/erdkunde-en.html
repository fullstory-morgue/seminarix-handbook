Seminarix is a Live-medium based on sidux, which is developed for the education of teachers.

   Geography Section

Notes

In the geography section there are programs which support and/or run geographical and temporal analysis.

In all cases in which we found additional help on the Internet, we have linked it with Info under the program description(some of it in English).

Some of them are magazine articles, some are Wiki-Articles. Since we are not the owners of these Articles, we can not guarantee that these links will work when you depend on them to get help.

At the time of writing, all information is current.

In matters of legality, what is written in the Impressum holds.

Program Introduction

The programs can be started/installed with the help of the program-starter. The programs of this page can be found under the menu item "Geography". You can find the program-starter in the "Kde-Menu" in the sub-menu "Utilities", or you can just click on the program-starter icon on the desktop.

Marble

Marble belongs to the KDE-Edutainment-Project, which develops various educational programs for the KDE-Desktop.

After the first time it is started, Marble shows a globe. The cross in the middle points on Europe. Via Drag & Drop you can rotate the artificial earth around its axis. If this steering methods makes you dizzy, just place the mouse pointer at one of the edges of the window. With each mouse click, Marble moves the map in the direction shown by a white arrow.

The column on the left screen edge helps you with the navigation. With the buttons with the arrows you can rotate the virtual earth in the wanted direction, with the button that has a small house on it you can jump to the initial view point. With the Magnifying glass function you can zoom in/out at a specific point. You can also zoom using the mouse wheel.

Marble

SunClock

SunClock presents a map of the earth and indicates, at a given time, the illuminated portions of the globe.

At the presentation you can switch between a time window and a map window.

The time window shows a small section (and needs less working memory). The map window shows a big map and offers a list of further functions: local time, sun's possition, position of the moon etc.
