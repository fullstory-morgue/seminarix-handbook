Browser Interoperability

Imagine that you are sitting in a restaurant and that the waiter tells you after you have ordered that the dish you chose is not available to you because you do not meet the expectations.

It could be that this story is a bit far-fetched but in the Internet it is like this sometimes that you - depending on your chosen favorite browser - can not see some information. Reason: Your browser does not interpret some commands or does not interpret them correctly. The browser's programmers are of course interested in creating their "fan-club" or in maintaining it.

This is pretty much an endless fight, that we as Internet users do not need to care about - we just can not see some information.

Interoperability should, as producers of informational sites, interest us since we depend on everybody being able to read the information.

As far as that goes there is only one thing left to do: After finishing with the source code, on the local computer, check the pages to see if every browser interprets them correctly.

Only then are they copied to the Internet server.

This means:

The optimisation of the Internet performance for all popular browsers.
