Seminarix is a Live-medium based on sidux, which is developed for the education of teachers

  Physics Section

Notes

In the physics section you can find many programs that show the stars, star constellations and simulations. Obviously original physics programs are scarce.

Programs Introduction

The programs can be started/installed with the help of the program-starter. The programs of this page can be found under the menu item "Physics". You can find the program-starter in the "Kde-Menu" in the sub-menu "Utilities", or you can just click on the program-starter icon on the desktop.

Celestia

Unlike most software-planetariums, Celestia is not earth bound. It offers you a free flight through the galaxy with breath taking views of the solar system and for over 100,000 stars. A functioning 3D-Graphics driver is needed.

It is also available for other Operating Systems.

Celestia

 

Planets

The gravitational force simulator "Planets" is an easy, interactive program which simulates, planetary systems in a playful manner. Planets is suitable for demonstrating the effects the gravitational forces between multiple space objects.

The user interface has a simple design and because of that it is suitable for students in lower classes, that is for very young children, in a special "Kid-Mode".

The program offers many options, for example you can adjust so the planets will bounce or merge when they touch one an other, (the second option is well suited for demonstrating the creation of the solar system).

Planets

 

KStars

Kstars is the astronomy program, and part of the KDE-project: Kstars is a precise simulation of the night sky with stars, constellations, star clusters, nebula, galaxies, all the planets, the sun, the moon, comets and asteroids. Kstars provides comprehensive information for stars, planets and moons. You can view the starry sky as it is at a specific time and place. Kstars supports the connection and operation of telescopes.

KStars

 

Kworldclock

The program shows, at the specific time, where on earth it is dark and where there is light. Also the local time in all major cities can be presented.

The program can run both as stand-alone or in the KDE task-bar.

Kworldclock
